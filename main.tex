\section{Theoretical Fundation}
The exercise consists of determining the position of objects in space,
depeding on the distance between them.

Considering a matrix:
\begin{equation}
\bm{D} = 
\begin{pmatrix}
0 & d_{12} & ... & d_{1n} \\
d_{21} & 0 & ... & d_{2n} \\
\vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\
d_{n1} & d_{n2} & ... & 0    
\end{pmatrix}
\end{equation}

which represents the pairwise distance between the objects. The Matrix is 
symmetric.

The objective is to determine the position of the objects, in this case 2 dimensional, represented
with the matrix:

\begin{equation}
\bm{X} = 
\begin{pmatrix}
x_{11} & x_{12} \\
x_{21} & x_{22} \\
\vdots & \vdots \\
x_{n1} & x_{n2}
\end{pmatrix}.
\end{equation}

\subsection{Optimization Problem}
The objective is to find $\bm{X}$, so that the the distance between the positions
is approximatly the value of the $\bm{D}$ matrix.

Meaning, the objective is:

\begin{equation}
    \min_{\bm{X}}\limits h_{\bm{X}}(\bm{D}) = \sum_{i=2}^{n} \sum_{j=1}^{i-1} \left|d_{ij} - \lVert x_i - x_j \rVert \right|
\end{equation}


Since the absolute of a value is not a differentiable value, the l2-Norm is used.


\begin{equation}
    \min_{\bm{X}}\limits h_{\bm{X}}(\bm{D}) = \sum_{i=2}^{n} \sum_{j=1}^{i-1} \left(d_{ij} - \lVert x_i - x_j \rVert \right)^2
\end{equation}
